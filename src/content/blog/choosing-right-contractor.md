Choosing the right building partner is the single most important decision of your construction journey. A wrong choice can lead to delays, poor finishing, and legal disputes. 

To help you choose wisely, here are **10 essential checkpoints** to verify before signing any contract.

## The Checklist

1. **Licensing and ISO Certification:** Ensure the contractor is registered and complies with national building standards.
2. **Detailed Estimates (BOQ):** Avoid lump-sum quotes. Ask for a detailed Bill of Quantities showing material brands and volumes.
3. **Written Timelines:** The agreement must include milestones and penalty clauses for late delivery.
4. **Recent Work Portfolio:** Visit at least two projects completed by the contractor within the last 18 months.
5. **Team Credentials:** Confirm they have full-time structural engineers and safety supervisors on their payroll.
6. **Financial Stability:** Ask if they have active accounts and supply relations with top concrete, steel, and paint suppliers.
7. **Transparent Escrow / Payment Schedules:** Never pay more than 10-15% upfront. Payments must be linked to verified build phases.
8. **Material Warranties:** Ensure warranties on structural elements, waterproofing, and electrical/plumbing fittings are transferred to you.
9. **Safety Audits:** Verify that safety protocols are followed on-site to prevent accidental liability.
10. **Modern Visual Tools:** A contractor who uses 3D models and digital dashboards is always more organized than one relying on paper maps.
